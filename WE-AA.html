Internship: Les Apprentis d'Auteuil

History

Catholic foundation recognized as being of public utility since 1929, committed player in prevention and child protection, Apprentis d´Auteuil is developing in France and internationally reception, education, training and integration programs to give back to more than 30,000 vulnerable young people and families that she supports in France.
With the help of their international partners, it supports 25,000 young people and families in more than 32 countries around the world.

The Auteuil Foundation was founded in 1866 by Father Louis Roussel (1825-1897) and Father Daniel Brottier (1876-1936) who placed themselves under the protection of Thérèse de Lisieux and intended "to create, act and innovate for the service of the poorest and most destitute ”.

My Tasks

The tasks carried out during this internship were diverse and varied. They could be of order manual or intellectual and the skills acquired were linked to the different missions. Reminder of missions:

  • Reception of donors
  • Sorting of material donations received
  • Preparation of donations storage labeling
  • Shelving
  • Customer reception and advice
  • Customer sales

Manual tasks were carried out mainly in the reserve (place where the various products which are subsequently put on sale in the various stores), the reserve being separated into 5 parts corresponding to the different stores.

General Conclusion

Thanks to this internship and the missions that were offered to me, I was able to acquire a lot of social skills that will be of great use to me in the rest of my studies.
Regarding my goals, this internship allowed me to evolve, grow and learn more about myself, I feel less shy than before.

This internship also taught me that repetitive tasks are not the most suitable for me and that from a social point of view, we do not choose our work colleagues, which is why we must learn to accept others to achieve given goals
